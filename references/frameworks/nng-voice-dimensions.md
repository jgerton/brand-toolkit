# NN/g Voice Dimensions + Aaker Brand Personality + Jung Archetypes

Sources: Nielsen Norman Group voice dimensions, Jennifer Aaker's brand personality framework, Carl Jung's 12 brand archetypes

## Core Principle

Brand voice is not what you say, it's how you say it. A well-defined voice makes every piece of content recognizably yours, even without the logo. Voice should be documented as measurable dimensions with concrete examples, not vague adjectives.

---

## NN/g Four Voice Dimensions

Each dimension is a spectrum scored from -3 to +3. Every brand sits somewhere on each spectrum.

### 1. Funny (-3) ↔ Serious (+3)

| Score | Description |
|-------|------------|
| -3 | Humor-first, jokes in every interaction, memes welcome |
| -2 | Frequently playful, wit is a core tool |
| -1 | Occasionally light, humor where appropriate |
| 0 | Neutral, neither humorous nor particularly serious |
| +1 | Generally straightforward, rarely jokes |
| +2 | Consistently serious, professional tone |
| +3 | Gravitas in every word, formality required |

### 2. Formal (-3) ↔ Casual (+3)

| Score | Description |
|-------|------------|
| -3 | Academic, third-person, full titles, no contractions |
| -2 | Professional, structured, polished |
| -1 | Business-appropriate, slightly warm |
| 0 | Balanced, approachable but not sloppy |
| +1 | Conversational, contractions, first-person |
| +2 | Informal, like talking to a smart friend |
| +3 | Slang welcome, sentence fragments, text-speak |

### 3. Respectful (-3) ↔ Irreverent (+3)

| Score | Description |
|-------|------------|
| -3 | Deeply deferential, careful, diplomatic |
| -2 | Polite, measured, considerate of all perspectives |
| -1 | Respectful but direct |
| 0 | Balanced, neither cautious nor provocative |
| +1 | Willing to challenge conventions |
| +2 | Openly contrarian, questions sacred cows |
| +3 | Deliberately provocative, breaks rules, challenges authority |

### 4. Enthusiastic (-3) ↔ Matter-of-fact (+3)

| Score | Description |
|-------|------------|
| -3 | Exclamation points! Energy! Excitement everywhere! |
| -2 | Clearly passionate, warm, encouraging |
| -1 | Positive but measured |
| 0 | Neutral energy |
| +1 | Practical, understated |
| +2 | Dry, let the facts speak |
| +3 | Clinical precision, zero embellishment |

## How to Score Dimensions

### Exercise
1. Gather 5-10 examples of existing brand content (if available)
2. For each dimension, place your brand on the spectrum
3. Get agreement from 3+ stakeholders
4. Document with examples at your chosen score AND at scores you're NOT

### The "NOT" Exercise (Critical for Anti-Slop)

For each dimension, write the same message in your voice AND in voices you explicitly reject:

**Example for a brand scored as Casual (+2), Enthusiastic (-1):**

On-brand: "Here's the thing: most email tools make you fight the interface before you can write a single word. Ours gets out of your way."

Off-brand (too formal): "Our email composition platform has been engineered to minimize interface friction, enabling rapid content creation."

Off-brand (too enthusiastic): "OMG, you're going to LOVE how fast you can write emails with us! It's absolutely game-changing!!"

The off-brand examples are the core anti-slop mechanism. They show what NOT to do.

## Jennifer Aaker's Brand Personality Dimensions

Five personality dimensions, each with facets:

### 1. Sincerity
- Down-to-earth, honest, wholesome, cheerful
- Brands: Coca-Cola, Hallmark, Patagonia

### 2. Excitement
- Daring, spirited, imaginative, up-to-date
- Brands: Red Bull, GoPro, Tesla

### 3. Competence
- Reliable, intelligent, successful
- Brands: Microsoft, Volvo, McKinsey

### 4. Sophistication
- Upper-class, charming, glamorous
- Brands: Chanel, Rolex, Mercedes-Benz

### 5. Ruggedness
- Outdoorsy, tough, athletic
- Brands: Jeep, Timberland, The North Face

### Exercise
Pick the PRIMARY dimension and one SECONDARY. Having more than two dilutes the personality.

## Jung's 12 Brand Archetypes

| Archetype | Motivation | Brand Examples | Voice Tendency |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| **Innocent** | Safety, simplicity | Dove, Coca-Cola | Warm, optimistic, simple |
| **Explorer** | Freedom, discovery | Patagonia, REI | Adventurous, independent |
| **Sage** | Knowledge, truth | Google, BBC | Informed, analytical, guiding |
| **Hero** | Mastery, courage | Nike, FedEx | Bold, confident, achievement |
| **Outlaw** | Liberation, revolution | Harley-Davidson, Virgin | Rebellious, disruptive |
| **Magician** | Transformation | Apple, Disney | Visionary, inspiring |
| **Regular Guy/Gal** | Belonging | IKEA, Target | Relatable, down-to-earth |
| **Lover** | Intimacy, experience | Victoria's Secret, Godiva | Sensual, passionate, indulgent |
| **Jester** | Joy, fun | Old Spice, M&M's | Humorous, irreverent, playful |
| **Caregiver** | Service, protection | Johnson & Johnson, TOMS | Nurturing, generous, supportive |
| **Creator** | Innovation, expression | Adobe, LEGO | Imaginative, expressive, perfectionist |
| **Ruler** | Control, stability | Mercedes, Rolex | Authoritative, refined, commanding |

### Exercise
Pick ONE primary archetype. This becomes the character behind your voice. The archetype should align with your audience's aspirations or self-image.

## Voice Documentation Template

### Voice Summary Card

```
Brand: [name]
Primary Personality: [Aaker dimension]
Archetype: [Jung archetype]

Dimensions:
  Funny ←——[X]——→ Serious     (score: ___)
  Formal ←——[X]——→ Casual     (score: ___)
  Respectful ←——[X]——→ Irreverent (score: ___)
  Enthusiastic ←——[X]——→ Matter-of-fact (score: ___)

We sound like: [2-3 reference voices, e.g., "a smart friend who works in the industry"]
We do NOT sound like: [2-3 anti-references, e.g., "a corporate press release" or "a hype-man"]
```

### Vocabulary Lists

**Use these words:**
- [Words that embody your voice]

**Avoid these words:**
- [Words that contradict your voice]
- [Plus all anti-slop banned generics]

### Example Pairs (On-Brand vs Off-Brand)

For each common content type (headline, product description, error message, email, social post), write:
1. The on-brand version
2. The off-brand version (too formal/casual/serious/etc.)

This is the single most useful voice artifact. Writers reference examples more than rules.

## Business-Type Adaptations

### SaaS
- Voice often leans Competence + Sage archetype
- Common trap: sounding like every other SaaS ("we empower teams to...")
- Fix: add a distinctive secondary dimension (humor, irreverence, extreme casualness)

### Local Service
- Voice often leans Sincerity + Caregiver
- Common trap: generic friendly ("we're passionate about serving our community")
- Fix: use specific local references, real personality, neighborhood identity

### Content Creator
- Voice IS the product. The creator's natural voice is the brand.
- Common trap: copying another creator's voice
- Fix: document YOUR voice quirks, speech patterns, opinions. Amplify what's already there.

### Ecommerce
- Voice varies widely by product category
- Common trap: product-description voice that's all features, no personality
- Fix: voice should tell the customer who they become by buying, not just what they get
